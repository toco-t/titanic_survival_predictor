Titanic Survival Predictor

Predict your chances of survival on the Titanic.

About the App

The Titanic Survival Predictor is a web application that uses machine learning to predict whether a passenger on the Titanic would have survived the disaster based on their personal information.

The model was trained on the historical data of Titanic passengers, including factors such as age, sex, socio-economic status, and cabin information.

By inputting your personal details, the app can provide you with an estimate of your chances of survival had you been on the Titanic.

How to Use the App

To use the Titanic Survival Predictor, simply fill out the form on the main page with the following information:

  • Age group
  • Sex (Male, Female)
  • Socio-economic status (Low, Middle, High)
  • Number of siblings/spouses aboard
  • Number of parents/children aboard
  • Cabin information (optional)
  • Port of embarkation (Cherbourg, Queenstown, Southampton)

Once you've filled out the form, click the "Predict Survival" button to see the estimated probability of your survival.

About the Data

The Titanic dataset used to train the machine learning model is a well-known dataset in the data science community. It contains information about 891 Titanic passengers, including whether they survived or not.

The dataset has been cleaned and preprocessed to ensure high-quality data for training the model. The model has been carefully tuned and validated to provide accurate predictions.
